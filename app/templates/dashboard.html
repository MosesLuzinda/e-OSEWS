One Health · Anticipatory action

Rift Valley Fever Intelligence Center

The e-OSEWS RVF intelligence center combines satellite signals, climate trends, field surveillance, and expert review to predict outbreaks up to three months in advance — turning data into early action for farmers, veterinarians, clinicians, and policymakers across Isingiro, Rubanda, and Kabale.

Objectives click any card

How our intelligence pipeline works click a step

  1. 1

    Sense

    Rainfall, NDVI, temperature, and satellite anomalies feed into our environmental layer for hotspot detection.

  2. 2

    Report

    VHTs, vets, clinicians, and TBAs submit structured cases via mobile and USSD — including GPS, syndrome, and exposures.

  3. 3

    Score

    AI risk-scoring and spatiotemporal anomaly detection turn raw signals into ranked hotspots and individual-level risk.

  4. 4

    Alert

    District focal persons receive prioritized alerts, with one-tap escalation, status workflow, and audit trail.

  5. 5

    Act

    Vaccination, vector control, clinical readiness, and risk communication launched before outbreaks escalate.

Why it matters

RVF continues to threaten food security, trade, and public health across Africa. Outbreaks often follow heavy rains and flooding, devastating livestock herds and the communities that depend on them. Timely prediction and early action save livelihoods and lives.
