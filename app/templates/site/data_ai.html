Analytics layer

Data & artificial intelligence

How e-OSEWS turns heterogeneous field data into risk signals and early warning.

Machine-learning risk scores

Structured human and animal events feed models that estimate individual- or cluster-level risk from syndrome combinations, species, gestational context, animal exposure, and geography. Scores are designed to be interpretable by district reviewers and to triage cases for follow-up—not to replace clinical judgment or laboratory confirmation.

Anomaly detection

Temporal and spatial anomaly methods highlight unexpected increases in compatible syndromes by parish or facility catchment, optionally weighted by environmental indices. Alerts can be generated when thresholds are crossed or when multivariate residuals suggest a departure from seasonal baselines.

Environmental data

Rainfall accumulation and vegetation condition (e.g. NDVI-derived greenness) modulate vector habitat and livestock movement. The system stores rainfall indices, NDVI, and temperature alongside events so analysts can visualise co-location of environmental stress and syndromic clusters—supporting early warning and after-action reviews.

Operational prediction horizon

Inspired by global RVF decision-support approaches, e-OSEWS is tuned for anticipatory planning by combining climate/environment trends, field surveillance, and analyst review to highlight potential hotspots ahead of widespread transmission.
