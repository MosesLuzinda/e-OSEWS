Human-centred design

Design of e-OSEWS

Co-design with end users, explicit roles, and technology choices that work when the network does not.

Co-design process

Requirements, forms, and dashboards were developed iteratively with district teams, facility staff, VHTs, and veterinarians. Workshops and field tests informed field names, syndrome lists, escalation language, and the balance between structured data (for analytics) and speed of reporting at the periphery.

User roles

VHTs

Community-level reporting of febrile illness in pregnancy, animal abortions, and deaths using simplified flows and USSD-style structured fields where appropriate.

Veterinarians

Livestock-focused syndromes, exposure context, and linkage to environmental risk layers for spatial prioritisation.

Clinicians & midwives

Facility-based structured event capture with gestational age and maternal risk factors aligned to surveillance case definitions.

District & national roles

Validation, alert management, maps, and exports scoped by district and permission level.

Offline-enabled apps

The mobile application queues submissions when connectivity is poor, retries with backoff, and uses idempotent keys to avoid duplicate records. GPS capture supports accurate mapping without manual coordinate entry.

USSD / SMS

Structured USSD-style pathways mirror key fields on the mobile app so community reporters can contribute consistent signals even without a smartphone.

GPS & remote sensing

Event coordinates ground-truth human and animal cases on maps; rainfall indices, NDVI, and temperature fields (from field observation or downstream sensing products) contextualise clusters for early warning and investigation.
