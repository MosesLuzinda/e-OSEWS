Electronic One Health Early Warning System

e-OSEWS links communities, clinics, and animal health for faster detection of Rift Valley fever–like risk

A district-centred platform that combines structured mobile reporting, USSD-style community signals, environmental context, and analytics to support timely public health and veterinary action in southwestern Uganda.

Project overview

e-OSEWS is an operational surveillance and early-warning initiative built around human and animal case reporting, environmental drivers (rainfall, vegetation, temperature), and role-based workflows for Village Health Teams (VHTs), veterinarians, clinicians, midwives, and district officers.

Objective

Improve the timeliness, completeness, and actionability of signals suggestive of Rift Valley fever (RVF) and related syndromes—especially pregnancy loss, haemorrhagic fever–like illness, and livestock abortion clusters—so that districts can verify, escalate, and coordinate response earlier than with paper-only or fragmented digital systems.

Purpose

e-OSEWS exists to strengthen One Health collaboration: the same system surfaces signals from maternal health, facility clinicians, community volunteers, and veterinary services, with shared maps, alerts, audit trails, and quality checks. It is designed for real field conditions—intermittent connectivity, varied literacy levels, and clear escalation paths—while remaining compatible with national and partner reporting expectations.

Feature stack

Offline-first field reporting

Mobile forms queue and retry submissions to preserve records in low-connectivity settings.

USSD-style community intake

Structured rapid signals support VHT and local reporting even when smartphones are limited.

One Health dashboards

Shared metrics for events, alerts, high-risk records, and data-quality flags.

Environmental risk context

Rainfall, NDVI, and temperature observations link climate patterns to outbreak risk.
