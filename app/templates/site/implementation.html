Study operations

Implementation

How the e-OSEWS intervention is rolled out, evaluated, and sustained with districts.

Implementation combines district planning, frontline training, and supervision loops.

Cluster randomised trial design

The evaluation uses a cluster RCT framework in which geographic or facility clusters are randomised to receive the full e-OSEWS package (training, devices or SIMs as applicable, dashboards, and supervision) versus standard-of-care surveillance and communication practices in control arms—while adhering to national ethical clearance and data protection requirements.

Intervention vs control arms

The intervention arm receives enhanced digital reporting, environmental integration, risk scoring, and structured feedback loops with district focal persons. The control arm continues routine reporting without the e-OSEWS toolset, enabling comparative measures of timeliness, completeness, and detection metrics.

Training cascade

Master trainers orient district surveillance and One Health leads; they in turn train facility teams, VHT coordinators, and veterinary extension staff. Training covers case definitions, device hygiene, consent and confidentiality, escalation to the district rapid response team, and use of job aids.

Community engagement

Community entry, drama and dialogue sessions, and feedback meetings explain the purpose of reporting, address rumours, and reinforce that reports contribute to protective action—not stigmatisation. Engagement materials are aligned with district health education units and veterinary outreach plans.
