Evidence

Pilot & evaluation

Designs and outcomes used to judge whether e-OSEWS improves surveillance performance.

Pilot evidence combines quantitative indicators with qualitative learning and field adaptation.

Interrupted time series

Where appropriate, an interrupted time series (ITS) analysis compares pre- and post-intervention trends in reporting indicators—accounting for seasonality and autocorrelation—to quantify step changes and slope changes associated with deployment of e-OSEWS.

Primary outcomes

  • Timeliness — median delay from symptom onset or event recognition to district-visible report.
  • Completeness — proportion of mandatory fields completed; proportion of facilities or parishes reporting in a reference period.
  • Outbreak detection — sensitivity and timeliness of cluster alerts compared to retrospective gold-standard line lists (where available).

RE-AIM and qualitative findings

RE-AIM dimensions (Reach, Effectiveness, Adoption, Implementation, Maintenance) structure scale-up planning. Complementary qualitative work captures trust, workload, gendered barriers to reporting, and collaboration between sectors—informing iterative improvements to training, dashboards, and USSD/mobile flows.

Evaluation journey

  • Baseline period: establish pre-intervention trends for timeliness, completeness, and detection indicators.
  • Implementation onset: rollout, supervision, and quality checks during initial adoption.
  • Midline review: compare intervention and control clusters; refine training and alert thresholds.
  • Endline and maintenance: assess sustained adoption, district ownership, and readiness for scale-up.
